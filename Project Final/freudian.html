FREUDIAN

 

Important Figures: Sigmund Freud (1856-1939) is the founder of psychoanalysis.

Topographical Model of the Mind (Iceberg):

Consciousness- The conscious mind consists of thoughts that are in our mind currently

Subconscious- The subconscious mind consists of thoughts that could be brought back to memory.

Unconscious- The unconscious mind consists of thoughts that one can not be aware of

Psychoanalysis- The process of bringing the unconscious back into consciousness

 

Freud thought that childhood occurrences molded our personality. For example, the occurrence of anxiety in the past is not in the conscious mind, so it becomes a problem later on in their adult life.

 

The Psyche: Entities that display a structural model of the mind

The ID (Instincts)- The ID consists of two biological drives which are Eros and Thanatos. These two drives are present in the unconscious mind because they are derived from the pleasure principles which are pleasures from satisfying basic instincts.

Eros- This is known as the life instinct; it helps a person survive and carries out basic functions such as breathing, eating, and sex.

Libido- The energy created by Eros.

Thanatos- This is known as the death instinct. It is the set of destructive forces such as energy expressed as aggression. However, Freud thought Eros is stronger than Thanatos and that is why we strive instead of self-destruct.

The Superego (Morality)- The superego develops when a child identifies with the same sex parent. This makes sure morals are followed.

The Ego (Reality)- The ego develops from the ID when one is an infant. The ego acts to satisfy the drives of the ID in a socially acceptable way. This balances out the id and superego when they come into conflict.
