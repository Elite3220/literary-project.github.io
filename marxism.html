Marxism

 

Marxist Criticism is an ideology that analyzes and criticizes from a different view point. This criticism is a mix of socialist view points and radical thinking for its time. Marxism criticism is centered on social class and how it affects the literature and the author. It was first started by a German Philanthropist by the name of Karl Marx.

Marxist Criticism theory was created in a time where civil unrest was growing and the rich were taking advantage of the poor. Most literary works reflect what is actually going on with the people and at that time people had nothing to do but work and think. From this came the new ideology Marxism it was developed by radical thinkers in Eastern Europe mostly in England and Germany. The reasons behind it being developed in those regions is because there was a significant class difference in both of those countries. The higher classes would take advantage of the inferior class below them to gain more materialist value in exchange for the welfare of the class below them. Later on other forms of socialism spread like communism this brought popularity to criticizing books with Marxism and blaming forms of oppression on social class.

The Karl Marx is a German philanthropist who studied politics and economics together and combined their philosophies to create a new socialism. The overall new ideology is named Marxism because its main creator is Karl Marx he created his socialist idea that later became made into literary theory. The theory itself was not solely created by Karl Marx he only made the political ideology he had help creating the literary element of the theory. The biggest influence on the theory was the friedrich engels he was a German Philanthropist just like Karl Marx. Friedrich added to the principles to recreate socialism into the literary theory he was not the only one he may have had the greatest influence on the literary theory but others had made additions to it. Later along the line Terry Eagleton had made a larger contribution to Marxist criticism in the form of refining it. Terry was a literature scholar and had refined the ideology of Marxist criticism to be better used in literature.
